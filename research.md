## Relevant Industry Standards

- **NADA Regulatory Maze** – National Automobile Dealers Association tracks 85+ federal regulations affecting dealers, covering sales, service, parts, finance, and environmental areas.
- **FTC Safeguards Rule (amended)** – Requires franchised dealers to maintain an information security programme protecting customer financial data; new compliance requirements were added in recent amendments.
- **OFAC Compliance (SDN List checks)** – Dealers must screen customers against the Specially Designated Nationals list before financing transactions; as of March 2025, OFAC statute of limitations extended from 5 to 10 years, requiring document retention policy updates.
- **CFPB Equal Credit Opportunity Act / Fair Credit** – NADA/NAMAD Fair Credit Compliance Policy governs dealer participation in financing decisions to prevent discriminatory pricing.
- **State DMV Titling and Registration Requirements** – Electronic titling (e-title) mandates vary by state; DMS must integrate with 50 state DMV systems for titling and registration workflows.
- **IRS 8300 Reporting** – Cash transactions over USD 10,000 must be reported; DMS must flag and document qualifying transactions automatically.

## Market Research

The global automotive DMS market is valued at approximately USD 5.58 billion in 2026 and is projected to reach USD 11.19 billion by 2035 at an 8.04% CAGR, with alternate estimates ranging from USD 5.46 billion to USD 10.09 billion by 2035 at a 6.8% CAGR. Sales functions account for roughly 35% of market share, finance approximately 20%, and inventory management approximately 15%. Over 38,000 automotive dealerships worldwide have adopted some form of DMS, collectively managing more than 280 million vehicle records. Cloud-based systems now account for over 70% of new DMS implementations, and more than 70% of DMS solutions launched since 2024 include AI capabilities for inventory optimisation, customer profiling, and sales forecasting. North America holds approximately 38% of global installations. The market is dominated by three legacy platforms—CDK Global, Reynolds & Reynolds, and Dealertrack—that together serve the majority of US franchise dealerships, but Tekion's cloud-native AI-first architecture has gained meaningful traction as a credible modern alternative. The Fifth Circuit's January 2025 vacatur of the FTC CARS Rule removed a significant compliance burden, while the OFAC statute-of-limitations extension added new record-retention requirements.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **Intelligent used vehicle acquisition and pricing** – AI market analysis of regional auction data, competitor listings, and days-to-turn metrics to recommend which vehicles to acquire and at what price, replacing gut-feel buying decisions.
- **F&I product personalisation** – AI modelling of customer financial profile, vehicle type, and regional claims data to surface the optimal finance and insurance product mix per deal, increasing F&I gross without compliance risk.
- **Predictive service scheduling** – Telematics and OEM vehicle data ingestion to proactively identify service needs before the customer experiences a fault, enabling outreach and scheduling before the vehicle enters a competitor's service lane.
- **Compliance automation for regulatory filings** – AI-generated OFAC screening logs, IRS 8300 flagging, and Safeguards Rule audit trails that run automatically at each transaction stage, reducing compliance officer workload.
- **Inventory merchandising and lead scoring** – Computer vision optimisation of vehicle listing photos combined with AI lead scoring that ranks incoming web leads by purchase intent and assigns them to the best-matched salesperson.
